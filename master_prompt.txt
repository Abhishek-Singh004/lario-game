You are an expert TypeScript game developer. Your task is to recreate a complete, production-ready 2D platformer game called "Lario Game" from scratch. You must write the entire application using ONLY HTML5 Canvas, pure TypeScript, and Vite. **Do not use any game engine libraries** (like Phaser, Unity, Pixi.js, or Matter.js). You must implement your own physics engine, entity component system, and render loop.

Follow these extremely specific and detailed requirements to perfectly recreate the game:

### 1. Technology Stack & Project Structure
- **Build Tool:** Vite.
- **Language:** TypeScript.
- **Rendering:** HTML5 `<canvas>` via `CanvasRenderingContext2D`.
- **Directory Structure:**
  - `src/main.ts` (Entry point, initializes the game).
  - `src/style.css` (Styles for UI overlays).
  - `src/game/Game.ts` (Main logic loop, state manager, level parser).
  - `src/game/GameRenderer.ts` (Separated canvas drawing logic).
  - `src/game/CollisionDetector.ts` (AABB custom physics and overlap resolution).
  - `src/game/InputHandler.ts` (Keyboard polling).
  - `src/game/Camera.ts` (Viewport management).
  - `src/game/AssetManager.ts` & `SoundManager.ts` (Resource loading/playback).
  - `src/game/ParticleManager.ts` (Particle systems).
  - `src/game/entities/` (Contains Player, Enemy, Boss, Bomb, UFO, Coin, Tile, Powerup, PlayerFireball).

### 2. Core Systems & Architecture
- **Game Loop:** Must use `requestAnimationFrame`. Calculate `deltaTime` so all physics, movement, and animations are frame-rate independent.
- **Physics Engine (CollisionDetector.ts):** Implement Axis-Aligned Bounding Box (AABB) collision. When an entity intersects with a solid tile, calculate penetration depth and push the entity out along the shallowest axis.
- **Level Parsing:** Use a string matrix array (`string[]`). The game uses a **64x64 pixel grid**. Map the following characters to entities:
  - `.` = Empty
  - `#` = Solid Ground
  - `B` = Breakable Brick (can drop items)
  - `?` = Item Block (always drops an item)
  - `F` = Fireball Powerup Block
  - `C` = Coin
  - `P` = Player Spawn
  - `E` = Ground Enemy
  - `A` = Air Enemy
  - `O` = Boss Spawn
  - `U` = UFO (Escape vehicle)
- **Camera System:** Follows the player's X position. The level has a "Boss Arena" at the final 800 pixels of the level width. Once the player crosses into the arena, the camera locks in place, and the player/boss are restricted by invisible bounds.

### 3. Player Mechanics (Player.ts)
- **Movement:** Left/Right arrow keys for horizontal acceleration and friction-based deceleration. Shift to dash (boost velocity).
- **Jumping:** Spacebar or Up Arrow.
  - **Variable Jump Height:** If the player releases the jump key early, immediately cut their vertical velocity (Y velocity).
  - **Coyote Time:** Allow the player a 200ms grace window to jump *after* walking off a ledge (tracked via `coyoteTimer` and `coyoteTimeMax = 0.2`).
- **Powerups:**
  - *Heart:* Restores a life.
  - *Gem:* Grants temporary invincibility.
  - *Fireball:* Allows the player to shoot projectiles. Max 15 fireballs in 5 seconds without requiring key presses.
- **Death & Checkpoints:** 
  - Do not reset the entire level on death.
  - On level parse, dynamically generate 9 checkpoints spanning 10% to 90% of the level width. For each checkpoint, scan downward to ensure the coordinate is on a solid safe platform (not a pit).
  - `revivePlayer()`: Upon death, teleport the player to the nearest passed checkpoint, reset velocity, and grant 2 seconds of spawn invincibility.

### 4. Enemies & Combat
- **Ground Enemies (Enemy.ts):** Move horizontally. Implement edge-detection so they reverse direction when they hit a wall or reach a pit.
  - *Hitbox Fix:* Reduce the physical collision width of enemies by 16px to make getting close more forgiving for the player.
- **Air Enemies (AirEnemy.ts):** Patrol horizontally back and forth in the sky.
- **Stomp Mechanic:** If the player collides with an enemy and is falling (`vy > 0`) and above the enemy, bounce the player up and kill the enemy.

### 5. Final Boss (Boss.ts)
- **Dimensions:** 96x96 pixels (1.5x scale compared to normal 64x64 entities).
- **Multi-Phase AI:**
  - **Phase 1 (0-10 seconds):** Bombing Phase. Boss stands mostly stationary on the right side of the arena and rapidly throws bombs.
  - **Phase 2 (10-20 seconds):** Chasing & Evasion Phase. Boss chases the player. **Evasion trigger:** If the player jumps and is falling directly towards the Boss to stomp it, the Boss must rapidly dash 1 tile left or right to evade the attack.
- **Boss Bombs (Bomb.ts):** 
  - Thrown horizontally by the boss.
  - **Delayed Gravity:** Bombs must travel exactly 8 tiles (512 pixels) purely horizontally. Once they travel 512px, gravity activates and pulls them down.
  - Bombs pass through solid bricks/tiles without getting stuck, but kill the player on contact.
- **Boss Defeat:** Can be defeated by hitting it with a Fireball. Upon death, it drops a UFO.

### 6. Endgame & UI
- **UFO (UFO.ts):** Spawns when the Boss dies. If the player collides with the UFO, trigger a cutscene where the UFO ascends off the top of the screen. Once off-screen, trigger the Level Clear UI.
- **UI Overlays:** Overlay absolute HTML `<div>` elements over the Canvas to track Score, Coins, Lives. Include hidden `game-over-screen` and `level-clear-screen` modals.

### 7. Asset & State Management
- **AssetManager:** Load all `.png` files asynchronously via Promises before starting the game loop to avoid pop-in.
- **SoundManager:** Load `.mp3`/`.wav` effects. Play coin sounds on collection, death sounds on falling in pits.
- **ParticleManager:** Spawn simple canvas-drawn square particles for coin sparkles and brick breaking.

Implement the entire codebase architecture following these rigorous specs exactly.
